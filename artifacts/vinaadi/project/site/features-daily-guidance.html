The four signals

What goes into a daily reading

The daily guidance reading integrates four sources of astrological information — each contributing a different layer to the overall picture of the day.

The result is a single score (0–100), a best window, a caution window, and a brief interpretation in plain language. Every signal is named, so you can see exactly why the day scores the way it does.

  • Vimshottari Dasha

    Your current dasha and bhukti period — the 120-year planetary cycle that defines the dominant influence over any span of life.

  • Gochar transits

    Today's planet positions relative to your natal chart. Saturn, Jupiter, Rahu, Ketu, Sun, Moon — each tracked against your lagna and rasi.

  • Tamil Panchangam

    Tithi, Vara, Nakshatra, Yoga, Karana — the five daily almanac elements that define the quality of each day in the Tamil calendar.

  • Moon nakshatra

    The transiting Moon nakshatra and its relationship to your birth nakshatra — including Chandrashtama tracking when the Moon crosses the 8th nakshatra from your janma nakshatra.

Clock times, not vague hours

Best windows and caution windows

Vinaadi calculates specific time windows for each day — not vague “morning” or “evening” guidance, but actual clock times based on panchangam Rahu Kalam, Yamagandam, Amrit Kalam, and Abhijit Muhurta combined with your personal chart timing.

The best window is the period most supported by the day's panchangam and your chart signals. The caution window is the period to approach carefully — not to fear, but to be aware of.

Why it stays current

Why this is more than a one-time tool

The daily reading changes every day because the transits, panchangam, and dasha context change every day. Dasha periods shift over months and years. Transits move over weeks and months. The panchangam shifts daily. Vinaadi integrates all of these together so you always have a current reading — not a static snapshot.

A chart tells you the structure of your birth moment. Daily guidance tells you how that structure interacts with today's sky.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

What is Chandrashtama, and how does Vinaadi handle it?

Chandrashtama is the period when the transiting Moon passes through the 8th sign from your natal Moon rasi. It is associated with increased caution in Tamil astrology. Vinaadi identifies Chandrashtama days, names them clearly, and includes them in the daily score — without amplifying anxiety or using fear language.

Which dasha system does Vinaadi use?

Vimshottari Dasha — the 120-year planetary period system tied to the birth nakshatra. Vinaadi tracks the main period (dasha), sub-period (bhukti), and sub-sub-period (antara).

Does the daily score account for my birth time accuracy?

Yes. If your birth time is uncertain, Vinaadi has a birth time rectification tool to help refine it. A more accurate birth time improves lagna calculation and dasha start precision.

Can I see readings for past or future days?

Yes. The dashboard includes a 7-day strip and the ability to navigate to any date to see the reading for that day.
